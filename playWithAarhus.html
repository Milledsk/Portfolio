Prototype

The prototype is designed for users to design closing in-store without losing the tangible qualities of trying and feeling the clothing before buying.

Interaction

Physical fabric and zipper samples is used to design the colors of the jacket part by holding the sampel to the corresponding interaction zone.

Technical setup

The prototype consists of a cardboard display with an arduino and RFID readers on the back, a physical jacket and a projector to illuminate the jacket.

Process

Designing and building Design Lab was an iterative process with initial empirical work, a small scale low fidelity prototype and lab evaluation, and finally two versions and evaluations of a high fidelity prototype.

The initial empirical work consisted of an online questionnaire with 185 responses. Hereafter two set of observations in clothing store were made; one where many different people were observed while shopping in a short amount of time, and one where two young women were followed around from they entered the store till they left again. The questionnaires and observations gave an insight in people's preferences while shopping and how they behave in the stores.

Building a low fidelity prototype was a fast way to get a valuable evaluation before spending many hours building the high fidelity prototype. After an iteration of the prototype it was evaluated and redesigned based on the data from the evaluation. The final prototype was evaluated outside the Peak Performance store in Aarhus.

Emperical work

The questionnaire and observations helped understand peoples preferences and behaivor while shopping.

Low-fidelity prototype

The first prototype was a small t-shirt with projection, and keypress to control the colors of the collar and main part.

Field evaluation

The final evaluation was outside the Peak Performance store on Storetorv in Aarhus where people passing by tried designing their own jacket.
